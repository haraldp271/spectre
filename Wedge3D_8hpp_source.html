19 /*!
20  * \ingroup CoordinateMaps
21  *
22  * \brief Three dimensional map from the cube to a wedge.
23  *
24  * \details The mapping that goes from a reference cube to a three-dimensional
25  * wedge centered on a coordinate axis covering a volume between an inner
26  * surface and outer surface. One of the surfaces must be spherical, but the
27  * curvature of the other surface can be anything between flat (a sphericity of
28  * 0) and spherical (a sphericity of 1).
29  *
30  * The first two logical coordinates correspond to the two angular coordinates,
31  * and the third to the radial coordinate
32  */
33 class Wedge3D {
34  public:
35  static constexpr size_t dim = 3;
36 
37  /*!
38  * Constructs a 3D wedge.
39  * \param radius_of_spherical_surface Radius of the spherical surface
40  * \param radius_of_other_surface Distance from the origin to one of the
41  * corners which lie on the other surface, which may be anything between flat
42  * and spherical.
43  * \param direction_of_wedge The axis on which the
44  * wedge is centred.
45  * \param sphericity_of_other_surface Value between 0 and 1 which determines
46  * whether the other surface is flat (value of 0), spherical (value of 1) or
47  * somewhere in between
48  */
